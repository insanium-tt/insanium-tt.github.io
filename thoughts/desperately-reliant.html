about now thoughts

Over the past two years I've significantly improved my "setup" through iterative, systematic reflection and improvement. Although my productivity, focus, and general output have improved significantly, I've realized in order to maintain my improvements, I've become desperately reliant on the tools I've curated. A (typical) mountaineer is reliant on his oxygen tank to get to the top of mount everest, right?

Given my environment, I suppose this is fine. The chances I lose everything tomorrow and have to rebuild from scratch are slim. However, the chances of something small breaking, throwing a wrench in my workflow, is much higher. At all costs, I want to avoid a Linux Evening.

I now have a vested interest in the reliability of the tools I curate, including my technology. As I continue to iterate and improve, I feel it is now required I consider reliability as a top priority.
