about now thoughts

When I think of form in mathematics, I think of the structure of a proof, the structure of a mathematical argument, or some sort of beautiful line of thinking that led to an answer. As such, form in mathematics is obviously a very subjective thing. Nonetheless, I've grown quite fond of the notion of good mathematical form.
Despite this, there is some sort of separation between my love of form, and me actually doing mathematics. When I read old proofs of mine, I see how rigid rough they are. My old proofs and mathematical arguments just don't flow. My new arguments aren't much better either.

I believe there is a profound beauty in form, and I've grown to be quite fond of the notion of good form, especially in mathematics.
